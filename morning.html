SURYANAMASKAR:

    Surya Namaskar, also known as Sun Salutation, is a revered sequence of yoga postures that pay homage to the sun. It is a dynamic practice that combines movement and breath, offering numerous physical and mental benefits.

    BENEFITS:

  • Dynamic sequence of yoga poses performed in a specific sequence.
  • Involves stretching and moving the body while coordinating with the breath.
  • Helps in feeling energized, flexible, and expresses gratitude to the sun for its energy and warmth.
  • Promotes balance, strength, and calmness with regular practice.
  • Tones the entire body and aids in weight loss.
  • Strengthens muscles and joints.
  • Improves complexion by enhancing digestive function.
  • Aids in better sleep by combating insomnia.

    PRECAUTIONS:

  • Individuals with a very bad or injured back should avoid Surya Namaskar, even if they have practiced it for years.
  • Beginners and those with health concerns should start gradually.
  • Consultation with a qualified yoga instructor or healthcare professional is essential if needed.

    AGE GROUP:

  • Namaskar can generally be performed by people of various age groups.
  • Intensity and number of repetitions may vary based on individual fitness levels and health conditions.
  • Children, adults, and seniors can all practice Surya Namaskar with modifications tailored to their specific needs and abilities.

VRKSHASANA (Tree Pose)

    Tree pose, also known as Vrksasana, is a standing yoga pose that promotes balance, focus, and mental clarity. It involves standing on one leg with the other foot pressed against the inner thigh of the standing leg.

    BENEFITS:

  • Improves balance, focus, and mental clarity.
  • Strengthens the hips and legs.
  • Enhances the functioning of the nervous system.
  • Enhances personal patience and calm levels.
  • Improves spinal health and body posture.
  • Stretches and tones the muscles of the thighs, calves, and ankles.
  • Boosts self-confidence and concentration.

    PRECAUTIONS:

  • Avoid practicing Vrikshasana if you have recent or chronic ankle or knee injuries.
  • Do not force the foot against the knee joint to prevent strain or injury.
  • People with high or low blood pressure should perform this pose with caution, modifying as necessary.
  • Ensure you have a stable surface and adequate space around you to prevent falls or collisions.

    AGE GROUP:

  • Tree Pose (Vrksasana) can generally be performed by people of various age groups, but caution should be exercised by individuals with specific health conditions.

PRANAYAMS

    Pranayams are a series of breathing exercises that promote physical, mental, and emotional well-being. They involve controlled breathing techniques that can help reduce stress, improve lung function, and increase mindfulness.

    BENEFITS:

  • Improves lung function and respiratory health.
  • Reduces stress and promotes relaxation.
  • Enhances mindfulness and mental clarity.
  • Boosts immune function and overall well-being.
  • Regulates autonomic nervous system activity.
  • Supports emotional balance and stability.
  • Increases energy levels and vitality.

    PRECAUTIONS:

  • Avoid practicing pranayams in extreme temperatures or polluted environments.
  • If you have respiratory conditions like asthma or COPD, consult a healthcare professional before starting pranayam practice.
  • Practice pranayams with awareness and moderation to prevent hyperventilation or dizziness.
  • Avoid forceful breathing or straining during pranayam practice, and listen to your body's signals.

    AGE GROUP:

  • Pranayams can generally be practiced by people of all age groups, but beginners and individuals with health conditions should start slowly and seek guidance from a qualified instructor.

BHUJANGASANA (Cobra Pose)

    Bhujangasana, also known as Cobra Pose, is a reclining back-bending asana in hatha yoga and modern yoga as exercise. It is commonly performed in a cycle of asanas in Surya Namaskar, Salute to the Sun, as an alternative to Urdhva Mukha Svanasana, Upward Dog Pose.

    BENEFITS:

  • Strengthens the spine and back muscles.
  • Stretches the chest, lungs, shoulders, and abdomen.
    • Improves flexibility of the spine.
  • Helps relieve stress and fatigue.
  • Stimulates organs in the abdomen, like the kidneys.
  • May help alleviate symptoms of asthma.
  • Improves digestion and helps relieve constipation.

    PRECAUTIONS:

  • People who have had recent abdominal surgery must avoid this yoga pose until the doctor advises.
  • Patients suffering from ulcers or hernia should not practice this asana.
  • Those having neck problems such as spondylitis must avoid this yoga pose.

    AGE GROUP:

  • Bhujangasana can generally be practiced by people of all age groups, but beginners and individuals with health conditions should start slowly and seek guidance from a qualified instructor.
