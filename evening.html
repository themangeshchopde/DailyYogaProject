    SUPTA BADDHA KONASAN

    Supta Baddha Konasana, also known as Reclining Bound Angle Pose, promotes circulation, enhances breathing, opens up the chest , releases the hips, strengthens and stretches the shoulders, and centres and calms the mind. It might provide not only physical benefits but also contribute to emotional well-being.

    BENEFITS:

  • Promotes circulation and enhances breathing.
  • Opens up the chest and releases the hips.
  • Strengthens and stretches the shoulders.
  • Centres and calms the mind.

    PRECAUTIONS:

  • Avoid if you have knee injuries, rheumatoid arthritis, or have undergone knee, ankle, or hip surgery.
  • Do not overstretch the inner thighs or groin area to prevent strain.
  • Practice with caution if you have lower back issues or sciatica.

    AGE GROUP:

  • Supta Baddha Konasana can generally be practiced by people of all age groups, but beginners and individuals with health conditions should start slowly and seek guidance from a qualified instructor.

    BALASAN

    Balasana, also known as Child's Pose or Resting Pose, has therapeutic benefits for anxiety, stress , depression, sleeping difficulties, and fatigue. It may activate the limbic system, which helps the body respond to intense emotions such as fear and anger by activating the fight or flight response.

    BENEFITS:

  • Therapeutic benefits for anxiety, stress, depression, sleeping difficulties, and fatigue.
  • Potential activation of the limbic system.
  • Relieves tension in the neck, shoulders, and spine.

    PRECAUTIONS:

  • Avoid if you have ankle, shoulder, hip, or knee joint injuries, or if you're a postnatal woman who has had a caesarean section.
  • Be cautious if you have high blood pressure or vertigo.
  • Do not force the forehead to the ground if it causes discomfort in the neck or spine.

    AGE GROUP:

  • Balasana can generally be practiced by people of all age groups, but individuals with specific health conditions should consult a healthcare professional before starting.

    VIPARIT KARANI

    Viparita Karani, also known as Legs Up the Wall Pose, alleviates stress, enhances self-healing, aids in better circulation, eases tension in the lower back and hamstrings, reduces muscle cramps and headaches, and improves blood flow.

    BENEFITS:

  • Alleviates stress and enhances self-healing.
  • Aids in better circulation and eases tension in the lower back and hamstrings.
  • Reduces muscle cramps and headaches.
  • Improves sleep quality and reduces insomnia.

    PRECAUTIONS:

  • Avoid during menstruation, and if you have serious neck and back pains or eye problems such as glaucoma.
  • Use caution if you have high blood pressure or heart problems.
  • Do not perform if you have a recent eye or head injury.

    AGE GROUP:

  • Viparita Karani can generally be practiced by people of all age groups, but individuals with specific health conditions should consult a healthcare professional before starting.

    PASCHIMOTTANASAN

    Paschimottanasana, also known as Seated Forward Bend Pose, strengthens the back and spine, relieves lower back pain and stiffness, reduces fatigue and insomnia, and improves digestion.

    BENEFITS:

  • Strengthens the back and spine.
  • Relieves lower back pain and stiffness.
  • Reduces fatigue and insomnia.
  • Improves digestion and massages the abdominal organs.

    PRECAUTIONS:

  • Avoid if you have recent abdominal surgery, slipped discs, stomach ulcers, diarrhoea, back or abdomen pain, or if you are pregnant.
  • Do not push beyond your limits, especially if you have tight hamstrings or a stiff lower back.
  • Avoid rounding the spine excessively, and keep the neck relaxed.

    AGE GROUP:

  • Paschimottanasana can generally be practiced by people of all age groups, but individuals with specific health conditions should consult a healthcare professional before starting.
